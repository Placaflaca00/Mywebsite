The transition period:My experience of disconnecting from technology and the challenges faced.

Day 1: A New Beginning or an End?

As I stepped out of the store, phone-less and feeling a mix of fear and excitement, I couldn't help but feel a void in my heart. It was as if a part of me was missing. But I knew deep down that this was the first step towards something greater.

Days 2 and 3: The Struggle is Real

The first few days were tough. I had to adapt to a new routine without my phone, and it was a struggle. I felt like I had lost my identity. But as I stuck to my habits, things started to get easier. The panic attacks subsided and I started to feel a sense of control over my life.

Days 4 and 5: Acceptance and Realization

By the fourth day, I started to accept the fact that I was going to be without my phone for a while. And that's when I realized how much time I had been wasting on social media and watching videos on YouTube. I started to watch television again, but avoided platforms like Netflix and Spotify. On the fifth day, I felt genuinely happy and content without my phone. I had the mentality that on the 6th, which was a Saturday, I would only use my computer for work.

Days 6 and Beyond: The Power of Time

The next few days were a blur. Time moved at a snail's pace, and it felt like a month had gone by. I had my computer and my gaming controller, but I didn't use them as much as I used to. I had achieved something that I never thought was possible. I had broken free from the shackles of technology and reclaimed my time and my life.
